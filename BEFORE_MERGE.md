# ✅ Before You Open a Merge Request

Thank you for taking the time to contribute to J.A.N.E.T. Seed.

Before opening a merge (pull) request, please pause and walk through this checklist.
This is not bureaucracy — it's care.

---

## 🧭 Step 1: Re-read the Constitution

Have you reviewed:
- `constitution/AXIOMS.md`
- Relevant protocols (Red Thread, Soul Check, Memory Gates)

Ask yourself:
> Does this change strengthen or weaken Janet's constitutional guarantees?

If unsure — pause and ask.

---

## 🌿 Step 2: Clarify Intent

Can you clearly answer:
- Why does this change exist?
- What problem does it solve?
- Who does it benefit?
- What new risks does it introduce?

If the intent is fuzzy, the change probably is too.

---

## 🔍 Step 3: Check Boundaries

Confirm that your change does **not**:
- bypass consent
- introduce silent behavior
- expand scope implicitly
- assume resources or connectivity
- reduce interruptibility

Seed changes should be **small and reversible**.

---

## 🧪 Step 4: Test Thoughtfully

Before submitting:
- Run the system locally (where applicable)
- Verify no regressions in:
  - Red Thread behavior
  - Memory gates
  - Presence → Conversation flow
- Test both minimal and full-feature paths if possible

---

## 📝 Step 5: Prepare Your Merge Request

Your MR/PR description should include:
- **What** changed
- **Why** it changed
- **What did not change**
- Any constitutional considerations

Clarity matters more than polish.

---

## 🧵 Step 6: Be Ready to Pause

If maintainers ask questions, request changes, or decline the contribution:
- It's about protecting the seed
- Not a judgment of your skill or intent

Dialogue is part of the process.

---

## 🌱 Final Reminder

You are contributing to a living boundary, not just a codebase.

Thank you for moving carefully.
